## Tier 1 — Strong candidates

### 1. **sharmeats** (current working name)
- **.com:** likely available ✓
- **.eg:** likely available ✓
- **.app:** likely available ✓
- Pronunciation: SHARM-eats. Trivially clear in English.
- Pros: literal, descriptive, SEO-friendly ("sharm" + "eats" both highly searched), the brand tells you what it does.
- Cons: not particularly creative; vulnerable to being seen as generic; doesn't transliterate to Arabic well (شارم إيتس reads as English to Arabic eyes).
- Trademark risk: low (generic combination).

### 2. **sharmbites**
- **.com:** likely available ✓
- Pros: same descriptive merit, slightly more playful.
- Cons: "bites" connotes snacks, may undersell full-meal orders.

### 3. **redseaeats**
- **.com:** likely available ✓
- Pros: geographic + iconic ("Red Sea"). Expansion-friendly — Hurghada, Dahab, Marsa Alam all sit on the Red Sea.
- Cons: less Sharm-specific (could weaken the "we know Sharm" wedge); longer to type; "eats" feels American.

---

## Tier 2 — Shorter / brandable

### 4. **sayyad** (صياد — "fisherman" in Arabic)
- **.app:** likely available ✓
- Pros: Arabic word, evokes fresh seafood and the sea (strong Sharm association); short; one-syllable easy; works in both directions Arabic/English.
- Cons: tourists may not understand the meaning; doesn't say "food delivery" at a glance; needs a strong tagline.

### 5. **sahn** (صحن — "plate" in Arabic)
- **.app:** likely available ✓
- Pros: Arabic, short, food-evocative, brandable.
- Cons: same as sayyad — needs tagline to communicate function; "sahn" is unfamiliar to non-Arabic speakers.

### 6. **sufra** (سفرة — "dining table / spread" in Arabic)
- **.app:** ✗ taken (Porkbun)
- Skip unless we want a `.com` variant.

## Tier 3 — Other

### 7. **naamafood**
- **.com:** likely available ✓
- Pros: hyper-local (Naama Bay is the launch zone).
- Cons: too local — boxes us out of expansion to Hadaba, Nabq, Hurghada.

### 8. **yallaeats**
- **.com:** likely available ✓
- Pros: "yalla" is universally known across the Arabic-speaking world; playful; informal.
- Cons: feels like a Cairo/Beirut brand, not Sharm-specific; doesn't tell tourists "we specialize in your hotel."

### 9. **sharmyummy**
- **.com:** likely available ✓
- Cons: cute but unserious. Skip.

### 10. **sharmsouk**
- **.com:** likely available ✓
- Pros: "souk" (market) implies marketplace; Arabic-friendly.
- Cons: souks are about shopping, not eating; potential confusion with grocery delivery.

### Eliminated
- **sharmly.com** — taken.
- **lekker-eats.com** — taken (likely an existing brand).
- **sufra.app** — taken.

## Recommended shortlist (decide between these 3)

1. **sharmeats** — safe, descriptive, SEO-rich, instantly clear. Default if no strong opinion.
2. **sayyad** — distinctive, Arabic, sea-evocative; demands brand investment to teach the meaning, but rewards it long-term.
3. **redseaeats** — sets up expansion to Hurghada / Dahab from day one; weaker Sharm specialization signal.

## Decision checklist (before locking the name)

For your final 1–3 choices, run **all** of these:

- [ ] Domain registrar check: search `sharmeats.com` (and `.eg`, `.app`) on Porkbun (cheapest) or Namecheap. **Buy immediately if available** — even before final decision. Domains are cheap, regret is not.
- [ ] Instagram handle check: `@sharmeats` (or candidate). Reserve.
- [ ] TikTok handle check: same.
- [ ] X/Twitter: same (low-priority for this market but worth grabbing).
- [ ] Apple App Store name check: try `https://apps.apple.com/app/sharmeats` — if 404, name is free in App Store namespace.
- [ ] Google Play search: confirm no existing food app uses the name in Egypt.
- [ ] Egyptian trademark search at `egtm.gov.eg` — confirm not registered by another F&B business.
- [ ] EUIPO trademark search at `euipo.europa.eu` — confirm no EU conflicts (matters for Italian/German tourist marketing).
- [ ] Negative connotation check — run the name past a native Arabic speaker, a native Russian speaker, and a native German speaker. ("Does this mean anything weird in your language?") Famous failure mode: Chevy Nova in Spanish.
- [ ] Pronunciation test — say "I work at [name]" to 5 people. If 2+ ask you to repeat, the name is too hard.
