**Status:** #1 blocker. Phase 1 (code) cannot start until this seat is filled.
**Target start:** within 4 weeks of role being opened.

**Cash compensation:** EGP 35,000–60,000/mo + performance bonus tied to restaurant onboarding count and order volume.

---

## Why this hire blocks everything

Restaurant onboarding in Sharm is **hands-and-feet work**. It is:
- Walking Naama Bay door-to-door at 3pm before kitchen prep starts.
- Sitting across from a chef-owner, drinking shai, signing an LOI in Arabic.
- Dropping off a "menu photography kit" (ring light + tripod + brand briefing).
- Being on WhatsApp at 11pm when a restaurant rejects an order.
- Knowing which hotel concierge to bribe with what favor.

None of this can be remote. None of this is doable by the founder remotely. Hire first, then build.

## Target Profile

- **Egyptian national.** Speaks Arabic natively. Comfortable in Egyptian colloquial business norms.
- **Sharm El Sheikh resident** OR genuinely willing to relocate within 30 days (verify with a lease commitment, not a verbal yes).
- **F&B or hospitality background** — 3+ years. Examples that count:
  - Restaurant general manager (any Sharm hotel restaurant or standalone).

  - Ex-Talabat / Otlob / Elmenus / **Twista** account manager (highest-value background — already has the relationships).

- **Comfortable with founder ambiguity.** This is not a salaried job; the first 6 months are scrappy.
- **Has a car or motorbike + Egyptian driver's license.** Naama → Hadaba → Nabq is not walkable.

### Strong-pluses
- Russian or Italian language (the two biggest tourist segments).
- Existing relationships with 5+ Sharm restaurant owners.
- Existing hotel concierge contacts at any of the named premium resorts.
- Sales background (not just operations) — they'll be pitching, not just managing.
- Has run their own small business before — understands the cash-flow anxiety of a restaurant owner.
- Tech-comfortable — can navigate Notion, WhatsApp Business, Google Sheets, an admin dashboard.

### Disqualifiers (hard no)
- Demands a fixed salary with no equity — wrong risk appetite for Phase 0.
- "I can do this from Cairo and visit Sharm twice a month" — not the role.
- Only works in English — Sharm restaurant kitchens are Arabic and Egyptian-Arabic colloquial.
- Burned bridges with one of the four incumbents in a way that closes restaurant doors.

## Interview Process (4 stages, ~2 weeks)

### Stage 1 — Screening call (30 min, video)
- Goal: confirm basics. Sharm-based, Arabic native, F&B background, willing to be a co-founder not employee.
- Red flags: wants fixed salary, "I'll work remotely from Cairo," can't articulate why they want *this*, badmouths Twista/Talabat reflexively (suggests poor judgment, not insight).

### Stage 2 — F&B knowledge test (45 min, video)
Open-ended questions:
1. "Walk me through how Twista probably onboards a new restaurant. Where do you think they're weak?"
2. "If I gave you 10 restaurants to sign in Naama Bay next month, which 10 would you go to first and why?"
3. "What's a fair commission rate to take from a Sharm restaurant? What's too high? Why?"
4. "Tell me about a time you got someone to change their mind in a business setting."

Looking for: specific names, specific addresses, concrete numbers, real anecdotes. **Vagueness is the death signal.**

### Stage 3 — Field test (1 day, in Sharm — you fly in)
- Together, walk into 3 restaurants in Naama Bay. They lead the conversation. You observe.
- Goal: see them work. Do owners light up or back away? Do they ask the right questions? Do they handle "no" gracefully?
- This is the single highest-signal stage. **Do not skip it.** Pay their day rate even if you don't hire them.

### Stage 4 — Reference + offer (1 week)
- 3 references from past F&B colleagues or restaurant owners they've worked with.
- Specific question to references: "Would you let them run a deal with you again? Why or why not?"
- Make the offer in writing. Equity, vesting, cash, role title (Co-Founder / Head of Operations), reporting structure (peer, not direct report).
- 30-day mutual probation written into the offer — both sides can walk for any reason.

## What you commit to them (the other side of the deal)

- A real co-founder seat: equity, board observer rights, name on the cap table.
- You do all the engineering and product. They do all the on-the-ground ops. Clean division.
- A cash floor (EGP 35–60k/mo) so they're not living on equity hopium for the first 12 months.
- Decision-making authority on restaurant terms (within a guardrail framework you agree together).
- You commit to a minimum of 5 weeks per quarter on the ground in Sharm yourself — they shouldn't be solo.
- An exit clause if either of you walks: 12-month accelerated vesting if terminated without cause.

## What success looks like in their first 90 days

- **Days 1–30:** 10 signed restaurant LOIs in Naama Bay. WhatsApp Business number running with restaurant onboarding flow. One hotel concierge contact established at a named resort.
- **Days 31–60:** First 5 restaurants live on the admin dashboard (you've shipped Phase 1 code by now). First 3 contracted riders identified (partnership with existing courier or direct hire).
- **Days 61–90:** 20 restaurants live. Closed beta running. First 100 paid orders. They're the primary contact for any restaurant-side or rider-side issue.

If they're behind at day 30, talk. If they're behind at day 60, the role isn't working. Be honest fast.
