### SLIDE 2 — The problem (tell it as one tourist's evening)
Sharm El Sheikh hosts millions of tourist-nights a year `[VERIFY: latest South Sinai/Sharm tourism stat + source]`, yet a hungry hotel guest tonight faces:
- **No Egyptian SIM, no local number** → can't receive the driver's call → delivery fails.
- **No street address** — resorts, room numbers, and beach clubs don't fit address forms built for Cairo.
- **Language wall**: apps and restaurant staff work in Arabic/English; the guest speaks Russian, Italian, or German.
- **Payment wall**: incumbents assume local cards or wallets; tourists carry foreign cards and cash.
- Residents are underserved too: hidden fees, bloated catalogs, no trust in ETAs.
Incumbents (Talabat, Bringit) are Cairo-pattern apps stretched to a resort town. ✅ Nobody built for the tourist.

Sharm Eats is delivery designed around how people actually live and vacation here:
- **Hotel-room delivery, no phone call needed** ✅ — pick a verified hotel, room number, handoff at room/lobby/reception/poolside. Works with zero local SIM.
- **Beach & GPS pin delivery** ✅ — order to a sunbed.
- **5 full languages** ✅ — EN / AR (RTL) / RU / IT / DE, 100% translation parity.
- **Prices in EUR/USD/GBP/RUB** ✅, charged in EGP. Cash on delivery from day one; card + Apple Pay via Paymob built and gated for rollout ✅.
- **Trust engineered in** ✅: live driver GPS with photo + plate + rating, automatic credit if 15+ min late, flat 20–40 EGP zone fees, free-cancellation window, allergy profile auto-briefed to the kitchen.
